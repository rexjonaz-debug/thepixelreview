Last year, the global ecommerce business model hit $5.8 trillion. Dropshipping represents a growing slice of that pie. The difference between success and failure today comes down to knowing the right niches, choosing the right suppliers, and understanding your customer.

What is Dropshipping?

Dropshipping is simple: you sell products online without holding inventory. A customer buys from your store, you purchase from a supplier, and they ship directly to the customer. You keep the difference as profit.

How to Start (7 Steps)

  1. Pick Your Niche: Choose something you understand or care about
  2. Research Suppliers: Find 10-20 products, check costs and shipping times
  3. Create Shopify Store: $29-99/month, professional setup in 6 hours
  4. Install Apps: Use Oberlo to connect suppliers and automate orders
  5. Set Pricing: 2-3x markup minimum (buy $20, sell $59)
  6. Launch Ads: Start with $5-10/day Facebook ads
  7. Optimize & Scale: Kill losers, scale winners

Common Mistakes (And How to Avoid Them)

Mistake 1: Choosing a niche you don't understand

Your descriptions will suck. Your ads will miss. Pick a niche where you've spent money before.

Mistake 2: Launching with 50+ products

You spread your budget thin and confuse customers. Start with 10-15 products only.

Mistake 3: Ignoring customer service

Respond within 4 hours to every message. Good service turns complaints into loyal customers.

Mistake 4: Selling products you don't believe in

Garbage products = chargebacks and refunds. Order the product yourself first.

Mistake 5: Spending only $3/day on ads

You need real data. Commit $10-20/day for 2-4 weeks minimum to make informed decisions.

Is It Still Profitable in 2024?

Yes. But it's changed. The winners are now specialists, not generalists. Instead of "we sell everything," it's "we're the authority on X for people who care about X."

Also, repeat customers matter now more than ever. First-time margins are thin. Return customers are where the real profit is.

You need depth. Understanding your customer's worries, lifestyle, and psychographics separates $1K/month stores from $10K/month stores.

FAQ

Q: How long until my first sale?

A: 1-2 weeks with ads, longer with organic. I've seen 3 days and 3 weeks. Most land in the 1-2 week range.

Q: What if my supplier doesn't fulfill?

A: Refund the customer immediately. Take the loss. Your reputation matters more than one sale. Have backups for every top product.

Q: Can I do this part-time?

A: Yes. You can manage a store in 5-10 hours weekly once it's set up. Won't grow fast, but it works alongside a job.

Q: Do I need a business license?

A: Generally yes. $50-200, takes a few hours. Also required for sales tax. Talk to a CPA for your state.

Q: Shopify or Wix?

A: Shopify for dropshipping. Wix for services. WooCommerce if you want more control. If you're asking, Shopify is your answer.

Q: Can I dropship from China?

A: Yes. AliExpress, Alibaba, DHgate. Shipping is 7-30 days. US suppliers are faster but more expensive.

Q: What's profit per sale?

A: Buy $20, sell $59. After fees and ads, you net $15-20 per sale. Small until you're doing 100+ sales monthly.

The Bottom Line

Dropshipping in 2024 is viable. It's not get-rich-quick. It's a legitimate business that takes work, testing, and patience.

The winners understand their niche deeply. They treat customers as people, not transactions. They optimize based on data, not assumptions. And they're willing to fail before they figure it out.

You don't need a massive budget or technical skills. You need curiosity, persistence, and willingness to learn by doing.

Pick a niche. Launch a store. Start with small ad budget. Learn what the market wants. Iterate.

That's literally how every successful dropshipper I know started.
