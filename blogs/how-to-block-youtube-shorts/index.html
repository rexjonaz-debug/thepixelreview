Why Block YouTube Shorts?

YouTube Shorts is designed to be addictive—autoplay, infinite scrolling, algorithm recommendations. People disable Shorts to: reduce distractions, limit kids' screen time, improve productivity, remove inappropriate content, or simply regain focus. Whatever your reason, these methods work.

🔧 How to Block YouTube Shorts: All Devices

Method 1: How to Disable YouTube Shorts on Android

Blocking Shorts on Android is straightforward. You have two options: disable via YouTube settings or use extensions if accessing via browser.

1

Open YouTube App

Launch the YouTube app and tap your profile icon (top right)

2

Go to Settings

Tap "Settings" from the dropdown menu

3

Find General Settings

Look for "General" or "Manage all data" options

4

Disable Shorts

Toggle off "YouTube Shorts" if the option appears

Note: YouTube doesn't always show this toggle. If you don't see it, the app doesn't have a native disable option yet. Alternative: Use third-party apps or extensions that block Shorts content.

💡 Pro Tip: If YouTube removes the Shorts tab but you still see Shorts recommendations, clear your YouTube history and stop watching them. YouTube's algorithm learns your behavior.

Method 2: Block YouTube Shorts Permanently on iPhone/iPad

iOS has limited native options to turn off YouTube Shorts, but you can use Screen Time and content restrictions.

1

Open Settings

Go to iPhone Settings → Screen Time

2

Set Content Restrictions

Tap "Content & Privacy Restrictions" → Enable it

3

Restrict Media

Go to "Apps" → Limit or block YouTube completely

4

Set Downtime

Create "Downtime" periods when YouTube is inaccessible

Method 3: How to Block YouTube Shorts on Google Chrome

The best way to disable YouTube Shorts on Chrome is using browser extensions. These actively block Shorts from loading.

1

Open Chrome Web Store

Search for "YouTube Shorts Blocker" or "Return YouTube Dislike"

2

Install Extension

Click "Add to Chrome" and confirm permissions

3

Configure Settings

Click the extension icon → Enable "Block Shorts"

4

Test It

Go to youtube.com/shorts – it should be blocked

Popular Extensions:

  • "Return YouTube Dislike" – Blocks Shorts + restores dislikes
  • "uBlock Origin" – Advanced ad/content blocker (can filter Shorts)
  • "YouTube Enhancement Suite" – Customizes YouTube experience

Method 4: How to Block YouTube Shorts on Smart TV

Smart TVs (Samsung, LG, Roku, etc.) have limited built-in options. The best approach is parental controls or using Family Link.

1

Access TV Settings

Go to your Smart TV Settings → Apps or Content

2

Find YouTube App

Locate and select the YouTube app

3

Disable or Restrict

Choose "Uninstall," "Disable," or "Parental Lock"

4

For Samsung TV Specifically

Settings → Parental Controls → App Lock → YouTube

Method 5: Block YouTube Shorts on Family Link

Google Family Link is the most comprehensive way to stop your child from watching YouTube Shorts.

1

Open Family Link App

Open Google Family Link on your device (parent account)

2

Select Child's Account

Choose the child's account you want to manage

3

Go to Apps

Navigate to "Apps & Games" or "Manage apps"

4

Restrict YouTube

Find YouTube → Block or restrict daily limit

5

Set Screen Time

Use Bedtime feature to disable YouTube access during certain hours

Best for Parents: Family Link allows you to monitor and control what your child watches in real-time, including blocking Shorts content.

Additional Tips to Block YouTube Shorts Effectively

  • Clear Your Watch History: YouTube's algorithm learns from your behavior. Clearing history reduces Shorts recommendations.
  • Don't Interact with Shorts: Every like/dislike tells YouTube you're interested. Stop engaging with them completely.
  • Use Incognito Mode: Browse YouTube in Incognito to prevent personalized recommendations.
  • DNS-level Blocking: Advanced users can block youtube.com/shorts at the router level using Pi-hole or similar tools.
  • App Limits: Set daily time limits on YouTube in your phone's built-in app management tools.
⚠️ Important: YouTube frequently updates its interface. If methods don't work, try again after clearing app cache or updating the YouTube app.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is there a way to disable YouTube Shorts permanently?

A: Yes, using browser extensions (Chrome) or Family Link (for child accounts) are the most permanent solutions. YouTube hasn't removed Shorts, so the app setting may not work on all devices.

Q: Can I stop my child from watching YouTube Shorts?

A: Yes. Use Google Family Link to block or limit YouTube access. You can set screen time, restrict specific apps, and monitor activity in real-time.

Q: How do I turn off the option for YouTube Shorts?

A: In the YouTube app, go to Settings → General → Toggle off YouTube Shorts. On the website, use a browser extension like Return YouTube Dislike.

Q: How do I ban Shorts from the YouTube app?

A: You can't completely ban it from the app, but you can: (1) Disable it in settings if available, (2) Use Family Link if it's a child's account, (3) Switch to YouTube Premium which has some customization options.

Q: Will YouTube remove Shorts?

A: Unlikely. Shorts is YouTube's response to TikTok and Instagram Reels. Instead of hoping for removal, using the methods above is more practical.

Conclusion: Take Control of Your YouTube Experience

Whether you're struggling with Shorts addiction or protecting your kids, these methods work across all devices. The most effective approach combines multiple strategies: disable where possible, use extensions, set app limits, and adjust your behavior (stop interacting with Shorts content).

Your screen time is your choice. Use these tools to reclaim it.
